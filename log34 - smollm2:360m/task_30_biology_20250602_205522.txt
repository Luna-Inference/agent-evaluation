Here's an overview of the molecular mechanisms involved:  
                                                                                                                                                                                 
1. Replication and Transcription: CRISPR-Cas9 works by utilizing an RNA guide, called Cas9 RNA, which serves as a template to synthesize a complementary RNA sequence. The target
sequence can be a gene of interest or a region of DNA that needs to be altered.                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                 
2. Targeted Endonucleases: Once the DNA has been generated, the Cas9 enzyme cleaves the target DNA at specific locations, generating double-stranded breaks (DSBs).

on the type of genetic modification being performed.                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                                 
   - Non-Homologous End Joining (NHEJ): This pathway repairs breaks in DNA at non-homologous sequences by incorporating random nucleotides into the gap. While efficient for

   - Homologous Recombination (HR): This pathway incorporates a new strand of DNA from another homologous chromosome to fill the break, thereby minimizing insertion or deletion 
mutation. the desired genetic modification.
