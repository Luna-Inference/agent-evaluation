center of the circle (the board or mat) to the axis of rotation (the skater's arms) reduces the gravitational force pulling them downwards, allowing the body and its momentum to
steer them in a more direct circular path.

only) and then comparing it to the other arm's motion, you'll see they will not be moving along the same curved paths, illustrating the difference in centripetal acceleration   
between the arms.                                                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                 
This phenomenon is analogous to a car tire spinning on a rotating road - as long as the tire stays within its groove (the path of travel for the center of rotation), it won't   
rotate at different speeds when pulled towards or away from the center of the circle (the road surface). However, in the case of the skater's arms and spin, this difference in  
centripetal acceleration leads to a greater increase in speed when pulling their arms closer to the body.
