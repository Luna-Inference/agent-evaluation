Cellular respiration is the process by which cells generate energy from the food they consume. It involves three main stages:                                
                                                                                                                                                             
1. **Glycolysis**: This is the first stage of cellular respiration and occurs in the cytosol of the cell. In this stage, glucose (a type of sugar) is broken 
down into pyruvate.                                                                                                                                          
2. **Citric Acid Cycle (Krebs cycle)**: This stage takes place in the mitochondria and is also known as the Krebs cycle or tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle.   
The pyruvate produced in glycolysis is converted into acetyl-CoA, which then enters the citric acid cycle.                                                   
3. **Oxidative Phosphorylation**: This stage occurs in the mitochondria and involves the transfer of electrons from NADH and FADH2 to oxygen. This process   
generates ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which is the energy currency of the cell.                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                             
Here's a simple representation of cellular respiration:                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                             
Glucose + O2 → Pyruvate + CO2                                                                                                                                
Pyruvate → Acetyl-CoA → Citric Acid Cycle → NADH, FADH2, ATP                                                                                                 
NADH, FADH2 + Oxygen → Water + ATP
