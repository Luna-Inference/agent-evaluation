A democracy is a form of government where power is held by the majority of citizens voting on laws and policies. It's governed by the will of the people, which are individuals  
who hold public office, through democratic processes that allow citizens to participate in decision-making and elect officials. In a democracy, the primary goal is to maximize  
the benefit for everyone, while also ensuring fairness, equality, and individual rights.                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                 
In contrast, a republic is a form of government where power is held by the leader or elected official choosing policies based on merit rather than popular opinion. A republic   
has a less democratic structure because it relies heavily on the consent of the governed to make decisions, which can lead to arbitrary or unprincipled laws and policies.

are many opinions about what should happen, it can lead to disagreements and ultimately result in compromise or even a winner-takes-all system where all votes are recorded      
except the first one from each candidate, rather than going through a referendum of 50% or more.                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                 
In summary: A democracy seeks to maximize the benefits for everyone while ensuring that those who hold power are held accountable and will be held accountable in the future as a
result of their decisions made by those they choose to govern them. A republic allows for individual freedoms, but it also means that power is not distributed equally among all 
citizens regardless of whether it's popular or not.
