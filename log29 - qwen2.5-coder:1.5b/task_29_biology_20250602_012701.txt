Cellular respiration is a complex metabolic process by which energy (ATP) is produced from glucose and oxygen. The process involves three main stages:

1. **Glycolysis**: This stage breaks down glucose into two molecules of pyruvate, releasing a small amount of ATP. It occurs in the cytoplasm of cells and is the first step in cellular respiration.

2. **The Krebs Cycle (Krebs' cycle)**: This cycle converts pyruvate back into carbon dioxide and water while generating energy in the form of ATP and NADH. It takes place in the mitochondria and occurs in the endoplasmic reticulum. The Krebs cycle is divided into four stages: citric acid cycle, electron transport chain, oxidative phosphorylation, and glycolysis.

3. **Oxidative Phosphorylation**: This stage uses ATP generated by glycolysis and NADH to produce adenosine triphosphate (ATP) in the mitochondria. It occurs in the inner mitochondrial membrane and is the final step of cellular respiration. Oxidative phosphorylation generates a large amount of ATP as well as the energy carriers FADH2 and NADPH2.
