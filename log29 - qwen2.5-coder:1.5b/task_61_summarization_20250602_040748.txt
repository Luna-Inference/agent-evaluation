The current study aims to investigate the relationship between sleep duration, physical activity levels, and brain function. We hypothesize that individuals who consistently

Sleep plays a crucial role in maintaining brain health by regulating brain chemistry and promoting the formation of new neural connections. Physical activity, on the other hand,

**Results:**

tasks such as memory recall, attention span, and decision-making were significantly higher in the intervention group compared to the control group. Additionally, the study 
identified correlations between sleep duration and physical activity levels with brain function, suggesting a positive interaction between these two factors.

**Conclusion:**

Our findings suggest that maintaining high-quality sleep and engaging in regular physical activity are essential for enhancing brain function. This integrated approach may 
represent new therapeutic targets for cognitive disorders. Future research should further explore the potential long-term effects of this combination on brain health and 
cognitive performance.
