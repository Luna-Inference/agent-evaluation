The heart consists of four chambers: the right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, and left ventricle[1][2][3]. These chambers work together to manage your heartbeat and      
ensure proper blood flow throughout your body[1].                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                 
## Function of Each Chamber                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                 
The chambers are organized into two sides (right and left) that form two pumps providing blood flow to the pulmonary and systemic circulations[4]:                               
                                                                                                                                                                                 
**Right Atrium**: This upper chamber receives oxygen-poor blood returning from the body and pumps it to the right ventricle[1].                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                 
**Right Ventricle**: The lower right chamber pumps oxygen-poor blood to the lungs where it becomes oxygenated[3].                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                 
**Left Atrium**: This upper chamber receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs via the pulmonary veins and pumps it to the left ventricle[3].                                     
                                                                                                                                                                                 
**Left Ventricle**: The lower left chamber pumps oxygenated blood to the aorta, which distributes it to all parts of the body[3].                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                 
Blood flows through these chambers in a specific sequence: it enters the right atrium, passes through the right ventricle to the lungs, returns to the left atrium, and then     
moves through the left ventricle before being distributed throughout the body[3].
